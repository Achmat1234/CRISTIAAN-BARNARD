About me

Dr.Christaan Barnard was a South African cardiac sugeon remembered for performing the worlds first human-to-human heart transplantation. Born as the son of a Dutch missionary in the Karoo region of South Africa, he earned his medical degree from the University of Cape town Medical School.

Achievements

  • 1945-Obtained his MB ChB at University of Cape town.
  • 1967-Completed the first successful human-to-human heart transplant.
    • 1961Christiaan was appointed as head of cardio-thoracic for the teaching hospital surgery department.

History

Born and raised in Bofort West, South Africa, 1922, Christiaan Barnard will forever be remembered as a hero. Thanks to his daring approach to take on the first human to human heart transplant, surgeons today can continue to save lives.

The transplant had successfully taken place in the Groote Schuur Hospital in 1967. Barnard transplanted the heart of accident-victim Denise Darvallminto the chest of 54-year-old Louis Washkanksky.Washkanksky regained full consciousness and being able to easily talk with his wife, but unfortunately died 18 days later of pneumonia.

Christiaan was also known to have experimented on dogs and later devaloped a remedy for the infant defect of intestinal atresia. This technique saved the lives of ten babies in Cape Town and was adopted by surgeons in Britain and the United States.
