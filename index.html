About me

Dr.Christaan Barnard was a South African cardiac sugeon remembered for performing the worlds first human-to-human heart transplantation.... Born as the son of a Dutch missionary in the Karoo region of South Africa, he earned his medical degree from the University of Cape town Medical School.








Achievements

  • 1945-Obtained his MB ChB at University of Cape town.
  • 1967-Completed the first successful human-to-human heart transplant.
  • 1961Christiaan was appointed as head of cardio-thoracic for the teaching hospital surgery department.


History

Born and raised in Bofort West, South Africa 1922, Christiaan Barnard will forever be remembered as a hero.
Thanks to his daring approach to take on the first human to human heart transplant, surgeons today can continue
to save lives.The transplant had successfully taken place in the Groote Schuur Hospital in 1967. Barnard transplanted
the heart of accident-victim Denise Darvallminto the chest of 54-year-old Louis Washkanksky.Washkanksky regained
full consciousness and being able to easily talk with his wife, but unfortunately died 18 days later of pneumonia.
Christiaan was also known to have experimented on dogs and later devaloped a remedy for the infant defect of
intestinal atresia. This technique saved the lives of ten babies in Cape Town and was adopted by surgeons
in Britain and the United States. Christaans
surgical career ended in 1983 due to devaloping rheumatoid arthritis in his hands.

He remained in Cape Town South Africa where he later became interested in anti-aging research. he also established the Christiaan Barnard Foundation during his remaining years, dedicated to help underprivileged children throughout the world. He died in 2001 at the age of 78 after an asthma attack.

He is greatly honoured today for his bravery in his surgical career.
